Data Collection Methods

Interviewer Script: (Contributed Liam, Narmer)

Hello my name is ______ and I am a (programmer, designer, etc) for an application aimed at improving the way students study and conditions in which they study here at Rutgers. Would you mind if I asked you a few questions about yourself and how you study, especially in groups?

Interview questions: (Narmer)

Name, Major, Year? Do you ever find it weird or awkward to ask random students in class for help? Why? Is it ever difficult to find other people to study with exactly when you need to? If yes, why? What are some factors that influence your decision to study alone or with other people? When working in groups, do you tend to get more work done with strangers or with friends? What platforms do you like to use to communicate with study partners?(Slack, GroupMe, etc.) and what do you like about these particular platforms? What things do you like to use to study with?(Post its, flashcards, whiteboards, etc.)? Is there anything you wish that was a bit easier when it comes to studying or finding people to study with?

Survey Questions:

What time do you usually study? How often do you study? How long are your study sessions? Are your study sessions planned or more sporadic? Where do you typically study? Do you prefer to study alone, or with a group? Are you open to studying with people you might have not known prior to a study session? Do you typically like helping others study? When studying in a group, do you prefer studying with the group in person or through digital means? When communicating with study partners, what method do you use most? How do you prefer to go about getting help when you’re having trouble studying class material? What is your major/minor? How old are you? What year are you?

Summary of Results (Contributors: Steven, Nate)

Interviews Regardless of if the students felt awkward asking random students for help or not, when tough work needs to be done, they typically search for classmates to work with - emphasizing the need to find others. The vast majority of the students mentioned they tend to have more productive study sessions with strangers. Students like to use materials such as post its, whiteboards, and text messaging to supplement their study sessions The bottom line is that students want to get their classwork done well and know that finding like minded individuals can aid them in achieving that goal. Survey Roughly 82% of students prefer to study after 6PM, with the average number of sessions being 2-5 times a week 59% of students say their study sessions are not planned Most of the students who say they typically study in their dorm rooms also indicate that they study alone - but also indicated they would like to interact with other students. 94% students prefer to study in person rather than over the internet A majority of students prefer to communicate with each other through texting and other instant messaging platforms. More students would rather go to fellow classmates for help instead of emailing their professors. Most students are open to the idea of studying with strangers
